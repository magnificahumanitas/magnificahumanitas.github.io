The dignity of the human person remains the unshakeable foundation upon which every just society must be built and continually renewed in truth.

Technology serves the person only when it remains subordinate to truth and ordered toward the genuine flourishing of all peoples everywhere.

No instrument fashioned by human hands may claim the authority that belongs to conscience alone, for the heart discerns what mere calculation can never reach.

When the machine is made to mirror the mind, we must remember that the mind itself is ordered to a truth it did not invent and cannot revoke.

The measure of every age is not the power it wields but the tenderness with which it guards the weakest among its people and the stranger at its gate.

Let progress be judged by whether it makes us more capable of love, more patient in justice, and more faithful to the promises we have made.

A society that forgets the face of the poor will soon forget its own, for we are known to one another only in the giving and receiving of mercy.
